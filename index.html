How Recommendation Systems Work

This platform uses weighted Euclidean distance to measure similarity between songs. Here's how:

Step 1: Feature Extraction

Each song has four metrics: energy (0-1), danceability (0-1), tempo (BPM), and popularity (0-100).

Step 2: Normalization

Tempo ranges 60-200 BPM while energy is 0-1, so we normalize all to 0-1 scale. This ensures fair comparison—a 20 BPM difference shouldn't matter more than a 0.2 energy difference.

Step 3: Distance Calculation

We calculate weighted distance: distance = 0.3×(ΔE) + 0.3×(ΔD) + 0.2×(ΔT) + 0.2×(ΔP)
Where E=energy, D=danceability, T=tempo, P=popularity.

Step 4: Confidence Scoring

Lower distance = higher confidence. Confidence = 1 - distance. A score of 0.95 means 95% similar!

Why This Works

Music taste is multi-dimensional. You don't just want the fastest song—you want energy-matched, dance-matched, and popular tracks. Weighting reflects this.

Real-World Applications

Spotify, Apple Music, and YouTube Music use similar approaches (plus collaborative filtering for "users who liked X also liked Y").

Future ML Enhancements

With more data, we could:

  • Train embedding models to learn latent features
  • Use clustering (K-means) for natural mood groups
  • Implement collaborative filtering with user data
  • Fine-tune weights with user feedback
